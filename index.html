Founder and Player

Uxbridge Secondary School Ultimate Frisbee Team

Identifying the need for an inclusive sports team which required limited equipment and gave students of all abilities the chance to participate, I started a high school Ultimate Frisbee Team in the Fall of 2016. After getting school permission and enlisting a teacher's commitment to supervise, I organized announcements and purchased Daredevil Discs from Ottawa. Our first two sessions saw 18+ students participating! Starting the Ultimate Team brought up many roadblocks, but I created a plan and worked through each of the obstacles by gaining support—for example, the company providing the Frisbees was excited about our new school team and provided discounted Frisbees, some free Frisbees and stickers for everyone on the team. Also, I was told there were not any teachers available to supervise, but building on a relationship from grade 9, I persuaded my teacher to come out and learn about Ultimate. Finally, we had everything necessary for a school team! We had a strong turnout for the fall session and great success competing in divisional tournaments in Spring 2017. I'm looking forward to another great season 2017/2018.

Student Delegate

Vimy Ridge 100th Anniversary Trip, April 2017

In April 2017, I embarked on a ten day pilgrimage to Europe with fifty Uxbridge students to tour historic battlefields, graveyards, and museums in France and Belgium, and to participate in the 100th Anniversary of the Vimy Battle. In preparation, I connected with the stories of WWI veterans and visited with local WWII veterans before tracing their footsteps over the beaches and battlefields of Europe. I cannot describe the impact this experience has had on my life—my understanding of veterans, the horrors of war, and gratitude for my citizenship and freedom. My research gave me an awareness of the desperate conditions of war and the devastating consequences to all soldiers. Researching my great grandfather’s five years of service in WWII provided a connection to the places I visited. This experience continues as I am inspired to learn more about the sacrifices made by our veterans and my place as a Canadian in the world.
